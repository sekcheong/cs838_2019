Snicket tops US box office chart. 

The film adaptation of Lemony Snicket novels has topped the North America box office chart, displacing Ocean's 12.

A Series of Unfortunate Events, starring <name>Jim Carrey</name>, took $30.2m (£15.5m) in its debut weekend. Ocean's 12 fell to number two while new entry Spanglish entered the chart at number three, taking $9m (£4.6m). A Series of Unfortunate Events also stars Scottish comedian <name>Billy Connelly</name>, while Carrey takes on a number of different roles in the surreal film. The only other new entry in the top 10 was re-<name>make,</name> Flight of the Phoenix, at number eight, starring <name>Dennis Quaid</name>. Carrey entertained crowds at the UK premiere of Lemony Snicket in London on Friday. The actor grabbed co-star <name>Meryl Streep</name> and whisked her around Leicester Square. Based on the cult children's books by author <name>Daniel Handler</name>, the film follows three children who are orphaned when their parents die in a fire. They are taken in by their wicked uncle <name>Count Olaf</name>, played by <name>Carrey</name>, who schemes to get his hands on their fortune.
