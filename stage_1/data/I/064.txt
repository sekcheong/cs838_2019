Connick Jr to lead Broadway show. 

Singer and actor <name>Harry Connick</name> Jr is to star in a Broadway revival of 1950s hit musical The Pajama Game.

He will play the supervisor of a US pyjama factory who has a romance with a union activist during labour unrest. <name>Jeffrey Richards</name>, the show's co-producer, said <name>Connick</name> was "an actor of enormous charisma and skill, a wonderful singer and a bona fide star". He has recently starred in hit US comedy Will and Grace as the husband of Grace, played by <name>Debra Messing</name>. The musical will open in November, said Mr <name>Richards</name>, who added that no other casting had been announced yet.

The original book by <name>George Abbott</name> and <name>Richard Bissell</name> will be revised by playwright <name>Peter Ackerman</name>, who co-wrote the screenplay for the movie Ice Age. It has a score by <name>Richard Adler</name> and <name>Jerry Ross</name>, and <name>Adler</name> is writing two new songs for the score, which includes numbers including Hey There, Small Talk and Steam Heat. <name>Connick</name> appeared with his band on Broadway in 1990, and he wrote the score for a musical based on Emile Zola's novel Therese Raquin, called Thou Shalt Not. It had a three-month run on Broadway in 2001.
