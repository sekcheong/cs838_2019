Vera Drake scoops film award. 

Oscar hopefuls <name>Mike Leigh</name> and <name>Imelda Staunton</name> were both winners at the 2004 Evening Standard British Film Awards.

Vera Drake - <name>Leigh</name>'s 1950s drama about a backstreet abortionist - was named best film and Staunton, who played the title role, was named best actress. Other winners included <name>Paddy Considine</name>, who was crowned best actor for his role in Dead Man's Shoes. <name>Bridget Jones</name>: The Edge of Reason was named Evening Standard Readers' Film of 2004 at the central London ceremony.

Leigh was presented with his winner's statuette by <name>Timothy Spall</name> and Staunton's award was announced by <name>Patrick Stewart</name>, during the glittering ceremony at The Savoy on Sunday night. Evening Standard film critic <name>Derek Malcolm</name> said: "He [<name>Leigh</name>] has never made a film that is better controlled and technically more secure... If this isn't one of the films of the year, I don't know what is." The <name>Alexander Walker</name> Special Award - which honours those who have made a supreme contribution to British film - went to <name>Tim Bevan</name> and <name>Eric Fellner</name>, the co-chairmen of Working Title films. The production company is behind films such as My Beautiful Laundrette, <name>Billy Elliot</name>, About A Boy, Shaun of The Dead and <name>Bridget Jones</name>: The Edge of Reason.<name>Simon Pegg</name>, who stars in and co-wrote Shaun of the Dead, won the 2004 <name>Peter Sellers</name> Award For Comedy. Other winners included <name>Emily Blunt</name> and <name>Nathalie Press</name> who were jointly named ITV London Most Promising Newcomer Award for their performances in <name>Pawel Pawlikowski</name>'s rites-of-passage story, My Summer of Love. <name>Pawlikowski</name> won the best screenplay statuette, while <name>Roger Deakins</name> won the Technical Achievement Award for his cinematography on The Village and The Ladykillers. Guests at the ceremony included Dame <name>Judi Dench</name>, <name>Kim Cattrall</name>, <name>Charles Dance</name>, <name>Bill Nighy</name> and <name>Colin Firth</name>. The awards, which were hosted by <name>Jack Dee</name>, are to be screened on ITV London on Tuesday at 2300 GMT.
