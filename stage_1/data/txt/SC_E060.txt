Snicket tops US box office chart. 

The film adaptation of Lemony Snicket novels has topped the North America box office chart, displacing Ocean's 12.

A Series of Unfortunate Events, starring <N>Jim Carrey</N>, took $30.2m (£15.5m) in its debut weekend. Ocean's 12 fell to number two while new entry Spanglish entered the chart at number three, taking $9m (£4.6m). A Series of Unfortunate Events also stars Scottish comedian <N>Billy Connelly</N>, while Carrey takes on a number of different roles in the surreal film. The only other new entry in the top 10 was re-<N>make,</N> Flight of the Phoenix, at number eight, starring <N>Dennis Quaid</N>. Carrey entertained crowds at the UK premiere of Lemony Snicket in London on Friday. The actor grabbed co-star <N>Meryl Streep</N> and whisked her around Leicester Square. Based on the cult children's books by author <N>Daniel Handler</N>, the film follows three children who are orphaned when their parents die in a fire. They are taken in by their wicked uncle <N>Count Olaf</N>, played by <N>Carrey</N>, who schemes to get his hands on their fortune.
