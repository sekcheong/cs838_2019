Connick Jr to lead Broadway show. 

Singer and actor <N>Harry Connick</N> Jr is to star in a Broadway revival of 1950s hit musical The Pajama Game.

He will play the supervisor of a US pyjama factory who has a romance with a union activist during labour unrest. <N>Jeffrey Richards</N>, the show's co-producer, said <N>Connick</N> was "an actor of enormous charisma and skill, a wonderful singer and a bona fide star". He has recently starred in hit US comedy Will and Grace as the husband of Grace, played by <N>Debra Messing</N>. The musical will open in November, said Mr <N>Richards</N>, who added that no other casting had been announced yet.

The original book by <N>George Abbott</N> and <N>Richard Bissell</N> will be revised by playwright <N>Peter Ackerman</N>, who co-wrote the screenplay for the movie Ice Age. It has a score by <N>Richard Adler</N> and <N>Jerry Ross</N>, and <N>Adler</N> is writing two new songs for the score, which includes numbers including Hey There, Small Talk and Steam Heat. <N>Connick</N> appeared with his band on Broadway in 1990, and he wrote the score for a musical based on Emile Zola's novel Therese Raquin, called Thou Shalt Not. It had a three-month run on Broadway in 2001.
